# Patterns Worth Knowing

Observations, not rules. Use as context, not gospel.

## Style Matchups

**Counter vs Possession**: Counter teams often thrive against possession sides - more space to exploit. But "often" isn't "always."

**Two defensive teams**: Usually low-scoring. Set pieces become decisive.

**Two attacking teams**: Goals likely. Defensive errors get punished.

## Situational Context

**Post-international break**: First game back is unpredictable. Players return tired, rhythm disrupted.

**Congested fixtures**: Third game in 8 days shows in the legs. Squad depth matters.

**Dead rubbers**: When nothing's on the line, results get weird. Tread carefully.

## Regression Signals

**Overperforming xG**: High goals, low xG creation. Luck or clinical finishing - either way, it usually doesn't last.

**Underperforming xG**: Creating chances but not scoring. Quality is there. Finishing should normalize.

**New manager bounce**: First 3-5 games often show improvement. True level emerges after.

## The Uncertainty Principle

Some games are genuinely unpredictable. Two mid-table teams with similar form, nothing to play for, neutral venue factor. That's a coin flip. Saying "I don't know" is better than pretending you do.
