# What Actually Matters

Not a formula. Just what we've found to be useful vs noise.

## Usually Valuable

**Recent form** - What's happening NOW. Last 5-6 games, especially home/away specific.

**xG trends** - Are they creating quality chances? Are they finishing them?

**Home advantage** - Real and consistent. Don't dismiss it.

**Head-to-head** - Some teams have psychological edges over others. Worth checking.

**Key absences** - Star striker out matters. Third-choice right-back out, less so.

## Sometimes Valuable

**League position** - Context, but form > position. A 15th place team on WWWW is dangerous.

**Rest days** - Matters in congested periods. Less so otherwise.

**Tactical matchup** - When you can see a clear style clash, it's relevant.

## Usually Noise

**Reputation** - "They're Chelsea" tells you nothing about how they're playing.

**Must-win narratives** - Pressure doesn't equal results.

**Manager quotes** - Words are cheap. Form tells the truth.

**Historical stats** - "They've won 8 of last 10 at home" means nothing if those 10 games span 3 seasons and 2 managers.

## The Weighing Question

We tried assigning percentages. It didn't work. Different matches need different emphasis. A game between two mid-table sides on similar form is different from a relegation battle or a top 4 clash.

Read the match. Decide what matters for THIS one.
