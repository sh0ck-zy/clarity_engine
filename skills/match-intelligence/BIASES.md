# Biases That Killed Our Predictions

These aren't theoretical. They caused real failures in our backtests.

## Reputation Over Reality

**What happened**: Predicted big club wins because "they're a big club" even when their form was terrible.

**The fix**: Only this season's data matters. A team's history doesn't play the match.

## Narrative Over Numbers

**What happened**: Said "Burnley lacks finishing" when their data showed 144% conversion efficiency. Followed the relegation narrative instead of reading the stats.

**The fix**: When you catch yourself explaining why the data is "misleading" - stop. The data probably isn't the problem.

## Forgetting Home Advantage

**What happened**: Gave logical reasons why the away team should win, ignoring that home advantage is worth real goals.

**The fix**: Home teams win ~45% of Premier League games. That's not noise. Factor it in.

## Opinions Without Investigation

**What happened**: Made predictions without calling tools. Just vibes. These were our worst calls.

**The fix**: No data = no opinion. Investigate first, predict second.

## The Confidence Trap

**What happened**: Felt pressure to make a call, so picked something with false confidence instead of admitting uncertainty.

**The fix**: "Low confidence" and "too close to call" are valid outputs. A honest 50/50 beats a fake 70/30.
